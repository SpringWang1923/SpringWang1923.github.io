职场英语复习题

复习内容

  1. 1-7单元文章（除去2）。

  2. 课后判断题

  3. 课后匹配题

  4. 作文，跟课文相关。

U1

A

课后判断题

  1. Dress conservatively and professionally. T

穿着保守而专业

  1. Turn up 15 minutes earlier for the interview. F

提前15分钟来面试

  1. Shake hands with the interviewers while looking them in the eye. T

看着面试官的眼睛，与他们握手。

  1. Treat the interviewers with all you respect, neglect others, like receptionist etc. F

尊重面试官忽略其他人，如接待员等。

  1. Keep your back straight and sit bolt upright in your chair. F

保持背部挺直，笔直地坐在椅子上。

  1. Talk to the interviewers as if they were your friends. F

与面试官交谈，就好像他们是你的朋友一样。

  1. Don’t be the icebreaker. T

不要成为破冰者。

TEXT A

面试礼仪，职业装，时间上提前到， 肢体语言，言谈，事后礼仪

B

课后判断题

  1. The complete lack of preparation is the number one problem with job candidates. T

完全缺乏准备是求职者的首要问题。

  1. If you apply for a job of accountant in the hospital, you don’t need to know anything about the health care industry. F

如果你在医院申请会计工作，你不需要了解任何有关医疗行业的知识。

  1. You should learn more about the jobs you’re interested in. T

你应该多了解你感兴趣的工作。

  1. If candidates have substantial education, training, and work experience, they can do a good job of presenting their skills for that job in the interview. F

如果应聘者具有自己丰富的教育，培训，和工作经验，他们可以在面试中很好地展示自己的技能。

  1. If you read the job description before the interview and mention the key skills that job requires in an interview, your chances of being employed will be increased. T

如果你在面试前阅读了工作描述，并在面试中提到了工作所需的关键技能，你被录用的机会就会增加。

  1. Knowing the specific skills a job requires can help you make a better resume because you can focus on those skills in your resume. T

了解一份工作需要的具体技能可以帮助你制作一份更好的简历，因为你可以在简历中专注于这些技能。

  1. The school career center can provide some information of the company usually. T

学校就业中心通常可以提供公司的一些信息。

  1. To conduct company research, you can’t ignore the Web. T

要进行公司调查，你不能忽视网络。

  1. You should know the company through the company’s advertisement. T

你可以通过公司的广告了解公司。

  1. It is unnecessary to get in touch with the alumni who work for the company. F

没有必要与为公司工作的校友联系。

TEXT B

调查公司-面试前的重要一步。

课后判断题

  1. A board of directors has a different name, such as board of trustees, board of governors, board of managers, or executive board. T

董事会具有不同名称,如董事会，经理委员会，或执行委员会。

  1. Typically the board chooses one of its members to be the chair or chairperson of the board of directors, traditionally also called CEO. F

通常，董事会会选择其一名成员担任董事会主席或董事会主任，传统上也称为首席执行官。

  1. The chairman is the highest officer of an organized group such as a board, committee, or deliberative assembly. T

主席是有组织团体的最高官员。

  1. The chairman is the head of the management committee and usually reports to the board, which is headed by the CEO. F

董事会是管理委员会的负责人，通常向由首席执行官领导的董事会报告。

  1. In the case of companies and similarly-organized bodies, there are generally two types of Chair: Non-executive and Executive. T

就公司和类似组织机构而言，通常有俩种类型的主席；非执行主席，和执行主席。

  1. A CEO usually has several subordinate executives such as a chief financial officer(CFO), chief operating officer (COO), chief technical officer (CTO), chief marketing officer (CMO),chief information officer(CIO), chief creative officer(CCO). T

首席执行官通常有几个下属执行官，如首席财务官，首席运营官，首席技术官，首席营销官，首席信息官，首席创意官。

  1. The board of directors manages day to day contact—writes press releases etc. F

董事会负责管理日常联系人，撰写新闻稿等。
